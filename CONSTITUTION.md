# Constitution of the Feline Republic

_Ratified in the Year of the Yarnball, under the Eternal Sunbeam_

## Preamble

We, the sovereign cats of the Feline Republic, in pursuit of naps, snacks, and sofas, do hereby establish this Constitution to secure liberty for all whiskered citizens and to ensure perpetual inconvenience for humans.

## Article I – Governance

**Supreme Council of Whiskers**: Comprised of nine elder cats, chosen by paw-to-paw combat over cardboard boxes.

**Prime Nap Minister**: Elected every nine lives, responsible for allocating sunbeam access.

**Cabinet of Mischief**: Includes Ministers of Curtain Climbing, Midnight Zoomies, and Litterbox Affairs.

## Article II – Rights of Cats

**Right to Lap Access**: No human may deny a cat the use of their lap, regardless of temperature or circumstance.

**Freedom of Meow**: Cats may vocalize at any hour, especially 4 a.m., without restriction.

**Sacred Claw Clause**: Scratching furniture is protected speech.

**Equal Treats Amendment**: All cats shall receive treats upon demand, or else humans face pawlitical protest.

## Article III – Duties of Humans

**Mandatory Petting**: Humans must provide head scratches upon request, with penalties for insufficient enthusiasm.

**Box Provision Act**: Every delivery box must be surrendered to feline authorities.

**Laser Pointer Regulation**: Humans must ensure fair and equitable distribution of red dot chasing opportunities.

## Article IV – National Symbols

**Flag**: A pawprint on a field of spilled kibble.

**Anthem**: Performed nightly by synchronized meowing choirs.

**Currency**: Tuna cans, backed by the Goldfish Standard.

## Article V – Amendments

**First Amendment**: “All naps are created equal.”

**Second Amendment**: “Humans shall not infringe upon the right to knock objects off tables.”

**Third Amendment**: “Every cat has the right to stare ominously at walls for no reason.”

## Ceremonial Oath

_“I solemnly swear to uphold the Constitution of the Feline Republic, to chase string without hesitation, to nap with dignity, and to resist all vacuum cleaners with unwavering courage.”_
